How it works

One Worker. Three outcomes.

A Cloudflare Worker sits in front of your site. Human visitors get your site exactly as it is. AI agents get a structured, cost-efficient, attributed response.

Step 01

Detect

Every inbound request is classified in under 1ms. 20 known AI agent patterns matched by User-Agent plus behavioral scoring for unknowns. Human traffic passes through unchanged.

Step 02

Serve

Agents receive a SOM — a compact JSON document with your content, author, publication date, and license terms. No ads. No scripts. Just information, at 4% of the original byte size.

Step 03

Track

Every agent request is logged — which bot, which page, how many bytes saved. Your dashboard shows real-time attribution so you know exactly which AI is reading what, and how often.

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to change my website code? +
No. Relay runs as a Cloudflare Worker — a lightweight script that runs at Cloudflare's edge, in front of your existing server. Your origin code stays exactly as it is. Human visitors see no difference whatsoever. Setup takes about 10 minutes via our CLI (npx relay-init) or manually in the Cloudflare dashboard.
Will this affect my SEO or human traffic? +
No. Relay only intercepts requests from confirmed AI agents — not from search engines like Googlebot (which is classified separately as "search" tier, not "training" tier). Human visitors and standard search crawlers pass through to your origin unchanged with zero added latency.
What if I'm not on Cloudflare? +
The current Worker requires Cloudflare — but Cloudflare is free to add to any domain. You don't need to move your hosting. You simply add your domain to Cloudflare (free), set your DNS to point through Cloudflare, and deploy the Worker. Your existing host stays unchanged. We're also working on Fastly and CDN77 integrations for enterprise customers.
Do AI companies actually respect the license policy? +
We enforce it at the network level — if you set "blocked", the Worker returns a 403 before any content is served. For attribution-required, the SOM response includes your license terms in every response, so AI companies consuming the data have clear notice. The RSL standard (supported by Reddit, Yahoo, Medium) is growing, and Relay is a natural enforcement layer for it.
How does the cost saving actually work? +
A typical article page is 150–200KB of HTML (including scripts, stylesheets, and ads). The SOM equivalent is 6–10KB of clean JSON. When an AI agent fetches your page, you serve 8KB instead of 180KB — a 96% reduction in egress data. At $0.09/GB (typical CDN egress), a site with 1M agent requests/month saves around $24,000/year. Your Relay dashboard shows this calculation live.
Can I see which specific articles AI is reading most? +
Yes — the Content tab in your dashboard ranks every page on your site by agent request volume, showing which bots read it and how much bandwidth was saved per page. This data is available from day one on the free plan, and gives you a clear picture of which content has the most AI leverage — useful for both editorial decisions and future licensing negotiations.
Is my content stored by Relay? +
No. Relay stores only metadata — which agent requested which URL, the size of the response, and the timestamp. The SOM content is generated at the edge and served directly to the agent. It is cached briefly in Cloudflare KV (5 minutes by default) to avoid redundant origin fetches, but never stored on our servers.
